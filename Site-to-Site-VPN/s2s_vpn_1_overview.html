What is a Site-to-Site VPN?

A Site-to-Site (S2S) VPN creates an encrypted IPsec/IKE tunnel between your on-premises VPN device and an Azure Virtual Network Gateway. Once established, on-prem resources talk to Azure VM subnets as if they were on the same private network.

It runs over the public Internet — no carrier circuits required — and is the most common hybrid pattern for small/medium workloads, DR, lift-and-shift, or a staging step before ExpressRoute.

Key idea: Two "gateways" negotiate a shared secret (PSK) or cert, agree on crypto, and build two IPsec SAs. All traffic between the two sites is then tunneled, encrypted, and authenticated — transparent to the workloads.

Encrypted By Default

All traffic in the tunnel is IPsec-encrypted (typically AES-256-GCM). Integrity via SHA-256 HMAC. Perfect Forward Secrecy with DH groups 14/19/20/24.

♾️

Dynamic or Static Routing

Route-based gateways support BGP for dynamic route exchange and transit. Policy-based gateways match static CIDR selectors — simple but limited.

🔁

Active-Active HA

Azure VPN Gateway can run two instances each with its own public IP. Combined with dual on-prem devices, you get ~4-tunnel redundancy for > 99.95% SLA.

💰

Pay-Per-Hour

Gateway is billed by SKU-hour. No per-GB egress inside the VNet. Far cheaper than ExpressRoute for small throughput (< 1 Gbps aggregate).
